Field of Application: Autonomous Systems
"ROBOTS, DRONES and MISSILES"

Our recent social transformations, maturity of the technologies or even the increase in embedded computing capacity have led to an increasing number of activities performed by autonomous systems such as robots and drones. This SA field of application aims to train the students in the common technological specificity required in autonomous systems by providing them an overarching and integrative vision of these systems. Targeted Knowledge and Skills This SA field covers a wide range of topic and targeted skills as the following:

  • Capacity to design an embedded system by taking into account all the specific applications constraints.
  • Global view of an autonomous system (architecture, formal organization, physical platform …)
  • Robustness and qualification conceptions of robots and drones.
  • Awareness of the interaction between the systems and human and/or environment by considering the ethic problems.

Course Organization

This field of application relies on an intensive and advance common cores and will be added at the end by a course on the design and operations of one of the following autonomous systems: robots, drones, or missiles.

Common Cores

Embedded Systems: design, modeling and architecture

Robots and drones are autonomous systems made up of a physical platform (sensors, actuators, processors) and software. The latter are organized in a logical architecture comprising of functional, supervisory and decision-making levels. This module deals with the main aspects to be taken into account when designing this architecture and the implementation of algorithms which constitute it, namely embedded systems on board the autonomous systems. The main concepts of this module could listed as the following:

  • Specification of functional and physical architecture by using SysML (System Modeling Language) [Cameo System Modeler, a commercial product of Dassault Systems]
  • Analysis of the operational safety of the architecture (Systems dependability and fault tree analysis ) [AltaRica 3.0 for Model-Based Safety Assessment]
  • System behavioral modeling and simulation (Petri Net and Model Checking) [Tina developped by LAAS]
  • Architecture for the execution of real-time embedded software. [Various sources]
  • Design/Modeling, validation and verification of critical software in robotics. [ROS, GENOME 3.0 developped by LAAS]

Perception and Navigation

This module introduces us to the famous area of GNC (Guidance, Navigation and Control). Autonomous navigation is the capacity for a mobile machine to move around in their environment without the intervention of a pilot. This capacity requires in particular that the machine perceives and models its environment, locate himself in place, and plans the displacement to perform. The objective of this module is to present the tools and methods to perform these functions, detailing in particular the localization, which plays a central role. The following concepts were introduced in a theoretical approach:

  • Introduction to autonomous navigation.
  • Foundation of robotics perception: proprioceptive and extreroceptive sensors, data estimation and fusion.
  • Localization in instrumented (outdoor) environment (GPS, GNSS) and in a non-instrumented environment based on proprioceptive (odometry and IMU sensors). Simultaneous Localization and Mapping technique (SLAM).
  • Environment modeling.
  • Motion planning and Trajectories tracking.
  • Today and the future application of autonomous navigation.
Most of the case study for this module were implemented in MATLAB and Python.

Decision Making and Deliberated Actions

Autonomous decision-making is the ability to choose and plan actions according to objectives and context. It is necessary as soon as that the autonomous system has to face a variety of tasks and that his activity cannot be scheduled in advance by the designer. It implements synthesis functions of the plan of actions and interactions, refinement of actions and supervision. This module is a brief introduction to the techniques used to staff an autonomous system of deliberation capacities. It will highlight also the ethical questions posed by robots (aerial or terrestrial). Program:

  • Introduction: Deliberate action as a guarantee of autonomy; models and representations.
  • Planning by state space search, Planning by decomposition of tasks.
  • Planning by constraints propagation (autonomous satellite)
  • Deliberation : adaptation, reaction, supervision, goal reasoning, learning.
  • Delegation, Human-Robot and multi-actors planning.
  • Human and Robot : authority sharing, strength and weakness.
  • Ethic : Ethic questions posed by the robots, in context of defense and security, with people, medical robots.
PDDL served as a tool for implementing and testing of our robot planning algorithms.

Parcours Drone

The Drones course aims to provide students with a multidisciplinary technical understanding of a drone system. At the end of this course, the student will have access to a good knowledge of jobs, missions, industrial challenges and technical constraints that allow to design a drone system. Practical sessions based on case studies will allow him to acquire good decision-making reflexes for the design of drones. The parcours covers drones for civil and military use, ranging from micro-drones to surveillance drones of MALE or HALE type, via combat drones (UCAV) Program:

  • Drone flight and Guidance:
    • Principle and controls structures
    • Advanced techniques of controls
    • Computer vision for drone controls
    • Case study/practical work: Implementation of “Control law and guidance for a drone”
  • ISR Surveillance Drone Engineering:
    • Introduction to ISR systems (Intelligence, Surveillance, Recognition) and feedback on a Medium Altitude Long Endurance (MALE) drone
    • Optronics / Infrared sensors and radars
    • Digital communications, coding and link budget
    • Introduction to micro-drones
    • Design and performance of missionary drones.
    • Case study : “Pre-design/Pre-dimensioning of an ISR drone ”
  • Unmanned combat aerial vehicle:
    • Operational concept and principles of design of a combat drone
    • Stealthiness and weapon integration.
The concepts learned in this module were implemented in MATLAB through those two case studies.
